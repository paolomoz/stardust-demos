Chapter I · The Maker

The high lonesome sound.

There's a thing in old-time music called the high lonesome sound. It's not sad exactly. It's the sound of one voice in a wide place. That's what I'm trying to put in a bottle.

The herbs grow in those wide places — the gaps, the balds, the deep coves — and I work in a small barn ten miles from anywhere. I sing while I work. I think it goes in.

— Sadie Greene, sole proprietor · Hot Springs, NC · May, MMXXVI

Chapter II · The Place & The Still

§ I Of the Place.

The Blue Ridge runs north along the western edge of Madison County. The gaps — wide passes between the ridges — keep their own weather. I forage where the rhododendron understory clears, where the sourwood blossoms in late June, and where the wild persimmon holds until first frost. Within forty miles.

§ II Of the Still.

The still is a copper kit a man in Tennessee sold me in 2019. He drew the schematic on the back of a feed-store receipt. I still have the receipt. The barn it sits in is ten miles from anywhere — a half-mile of unpaved road and then another. I sing while I work. I think it goes in.
