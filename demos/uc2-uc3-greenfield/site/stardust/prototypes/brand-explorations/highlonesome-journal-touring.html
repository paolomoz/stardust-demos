High Lonesome

— Notes from the road —

"There's a thing in old-time music called the high lonesome sound. It's not sad sad exactly. It's the sound of one voice in a wide place."

"That's what I'm trying to put in a bottle. The herbs grow in those wide places — the gaps, the balds, the deep coves. I work in a small barn ten miles from anywhere. I sing while I work. I think it goes in."

→ Footnote: foraged tonight — wild ginger from Wolfpen Creek (3 oz), butterfly pea flower from the bald (7 stems), eastern juniper (the bird showed me where).
