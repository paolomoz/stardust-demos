Chapter I · The Maker

Made by Hand. From A to Z.

"I grew up in a hollow outside Asheville where my grandmother kept a still in the smokehouse and a hymnal on the kitchen table. We never thought of them as different things. Twenty-seven herbs foraged from inside forty miles of my barn. Hand-distilled in the preacher's shed. Made from A to Z."

Wendell Greene, sole proprietor · Wolfpen Holler · Madison Co. NC · May, MMXXVI

Chapter II · The Place & The Still

Wolfpen Holler & the Still

§ I · The Place

Forty minutes outside Asheville. Rhododendron understory, sourwood blossom in late June, ramps under the leaf-mould in March. Everything in these bottles is foraged within forty miles of my barn. Nothing bought in a jug. The map of Madison County is, by chance, also the map of the brand.

§ II · The Still

Built in 2019 from a kit a man in Tennessee sold me. He drew the diagram on the back of a feed-store receipt. The shed it sits in I bought off the widow of a dead preacher for four thousand dollars. The labels are painted by hand, by me, in the same shed.
