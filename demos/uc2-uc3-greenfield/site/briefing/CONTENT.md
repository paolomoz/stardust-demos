# Holler & Hymn — Per-page content briefs

> Direction-led briefs, not full copy. Stardust expands each into prose during prototyping. The voice cheat sheet below is the load-bearing constraint — every page reads in Wendell's register or fails. Load-bearing nouns (names, places, products) are listed per page so Stardust uses them verbatim instead of inventing.

---

## Voice cheat sheet (read first; apply to every page)

**Narrator:** Wendell Greene. First-person singular everywhere except the legal/utility footer. Never "we," never "our team." Slow, plainspoken declaratives. Short sentences. Periods, not commas.

**Lift these phrasings verbatim** when the rhythm needs an anchor:

- "Made by hand. From a to z."
- "Foraged inside forty miles."
- "Nothing bought in a jug."
- "Twenty-seven herbs, eleven labels, one preacher's shed."
- "If you find one in a bar in Asheville, the bartender probably knows me."
- "Saturday night sin. Sunday morning hymn."

**Always over adjectives:** specifics (forty miles, 2019, twenty-seven), proper nouns (Wolfpen Holler, Madison County, Wendell), and process literalism (a copper still he built, a shed he bought off a preacher).

**Sacred/profane vocabulary** (use *sparingly* — each instance must land):

| Sacred | Profane |
|---|---|
| revival, tent, sermon, hymn, hymnal, pulpit, pew, altar, offering plate, deacon, brother, sister, elder, amen, blessing, salvation, pass the plate, take and drink | sin, backslider, dropout, no-show, jukebox, last call, headlight, tailgate, outlaw, hellraiser, ne'er-do-well, take a slug, pour one out, the long way home |

**Forbidden everywhere:** *we, our team, passionate, crafted, curated, purveyors, lifestyle, finest, unparalleled, artisanal* (the brand IS artisanal — saying so weakens it), adjective stacks of three or more, doormat-country slang ("y'all means all"), cocktail-jargon snobbery.

## 1. home — single-scroll narrative

**Purpose:** introduce the brand, the maker, and the line in one continuous scroll. The page from which every other page hangs.

**Hero beat:** large hand-lettered wordmark or seal · the tagline "Saturday night sin. Sunday morning hymn. Both in one bottle." · single CTA "find a pew" (scrolls to stockists) or "book a Saturday tasting."

**Sections (in scroll order):**

1. Hero — wordmark, tagline, one CTA.
2. The maker's voice — a 2-sentence pull from Wendell's origin paragraph + photo (Lane 1 imagery).
3. The holler — one paragraph on Wolfpen Holler + landscape image. Links out to "the holler."
4. The still — one paragraph on the process + close-up of copper still or mason jars. Links out to "the still."
5. The bottles — three featured products (PASS THE PLATE, TENT REVIVAL, THE BACKSLIDER) as cards with hand-painted label art. Links out to "the bottles" for full line.
6. Where to find — short list of 4–5 Asheville bars + "see all pews."
7. Footer — Wendell's email + phone, Instagram, mailing address, age-gate confirmation, legal.

**Load-bearing nouns:** Holler & Hymn · Wendell Greene · Wolfpen Holler · Madison County · forty miles · 2019 · PASS THE PLATE · TENT REVIVAL · THE BACKSLIDER.

**Voice sample (use the tagline as the page anchor):**

> "Holler & Hymn. Small-batch botanical liqueurs from Wolfpen Holler, Madison County, North Carolina. Made by hand by Wendell Greene, from herbs and fruit foraged inside forty miles. Saturday night sin and Sunday morning hymn. Both in one bottle."

**What NOT to include:** full product catalog (lives on bottles) · full recipe/cocktail list (lives on hymns) · full origin paragraph (lives on the holler) · pricing.

## 2. the holler — about / maker story / place

**Purpose:** anchor the brand in the person and the place. The only page where Wendell's full origin paragraph appears.

**Hero beat:** Wendell's portrait (Lane 1 imagery, pearl-snap shirt, no smile, real) + a single line as the page header. *"I grew up here. I still do."*

**Sections:**

1. The origin paragraph (verbatim from `../brand/BRAND.md`, the full "I grew up in a hollow outside Asheville…" block).
2. The place — Wolfpen Holler, Madison County. One paragraph on the landscape: rhododendron understory, sourwood blossom in late June, what the forty-mile radius covers (e.g. Mars Hill on the south edge, the Pisgah on the east, the Tennessee line on the north). Landscape image.
3. The smokehouse + hymnal anchor — short anecdote: the still in his grandmother's smokehouse, the hymnal on her kitchen table, why he doesn't think of them as different.
4. Why now — one short paragraph: he started in 2019 because nobody else was making this. Doesn't oversell.

**Load-bearing nouns:** Wendell Greene · Wolfpen Holler · Madison County · rhododendron · sourwood · Mars Hill · Pisgah · 2019 · the smokehouse · the hymnal · the preacher's shed.

**Voice sample (mid-page pull-quote):**

> "We never thought of them as different things."

**What NOT to include:** any line that begins with "Our story…" or "Founded in…" (corporate framing) · family-tree depth (we don't need three generations; we need the grandmother) · politics, religion-as-affiliation. Wendell is not a denomination; he is a person who grew up around hymnals.

## 3. the still — process

**Purpose:** prove the literalism of "made by hand from a to z." This page is where the brand earns the trust that the voice asserts.

**Hero beat:** wide photograph of the copper still in dim light (Lane 1 imagery) + a single line: *"Everything in this bottle was within forty miles of my barn three weeks ago."*

**Sections (each one a numbered process step with one specific detail):**

1. **Forage.** Specific seasons for specific plants (ramps in March, sourwood blossom in late June, sumac in August, persimmon after first frost). A real number: twenty-seven plants currently used across the line.
2. **Macerate.** Mason jars on a shelf in the barn. Maceration windows by botanical. One specific photograph or detail (the shelf, the labels, the jars).
3. **Distill.** The copper still Wendell built in 2019. Capacity (a real-feeling small number — e.g. 40 gallons per batch). Heat source detail.
4. **Label.** The shed he bought off a dead preacher (literal — "I paid the widow $4,000"). Hand-painted labels, one per bottle, by Wendell. Some imperfect on purpose.
5. **Bottle.** Glass from a guy in Knoxville. Bottled, capped, dated, shipped within thirty days of distillation.

Optional: a one-line botanical table at the end (the twenty-seven herbs/plants in plain Latin + common name).

**Load-bearing nouns:** copper still · 2019 · mason jars · sourwood · ramps · sumac · persimmon · twenty-seven · preacher's shed · Knoxville glass · Madison County · forty miles.

**Voice sample (close):**

> "I built the still in 2019 from a kit I bought from a man in Tennessee. He drew me a diagram on the back of a feed-store receipt. I still have the receipt."

**What NOT to include:** distillation chemistry lectures · "passion for craft" abstractions · molecular-gastronomy adjacent jargon · anything about "the team."

## 4. the bottles — full catalog

**Purpose:** the complete product line. The card grid lives here. The bottles page is where the cheekiness of the label-naming gets full air.

**Hero beat:** a row of four hand-painted bottles, irregular labels, on a barn shelf · one line: *"Six bottles. Four categories. Every label painted by hand."*

**Sections — one card per product, four categories:**

**Amari**
- **PASS THE PLATE** — Sunday-morning aperitif amaro. Golden, bitter, restrained. ABV: 24%. Lead botanicals: sourwood blossom, chicory, magnolia bark. Label art: an old offering plate, in rust-red on cream.
- **DEACON'S DRAUGHT** — Off-duty amaro. Medium-bitter, herbaceous, evening sipper. ABV: 26%. Lead botanicals: mountain mint, sassafras, black walnut. Label art: a deacon's collar undone.

**Bitters**
- **DRINK YE ALL OF IT** — Cocktail bitters set, three formats (aromatic, citrus, herbal). Per bottle 100ml, dasher cap. ABV: 44%. Reference: Matthew 26:27. Label art: King-James-Bible-style red-letter type, three matching bottles.

**Cordials**
- **THE BACKSLIDER** — Sweet cordial. Dark, plum-and-paw-paw forward, low-alcohol. ABV: 18%. Lead botanicals: paw-paw, wild persimmon, juniper berry. Label art: a kid sneaking out of Sunday school.
- **TENT REVIVAL** — Mountain herbal "amaro" — borderline cordial / borderline amaro. Smoky, dark, hot. ABV: 32%. Lead botanicals: staghorn sumac, eastern juniper, black walnut hull, ramps (spring batch only). Label art: a revival tent at night, hellfire-neon glow.

**Aperitivi**
- **THE NO-SHOW** — Low-ABV aperitivo. Sparkling-ready, pale-pink. ABV: 11%. Lead botanicals: muscadine grape, hibiscus, mountain mint. Label art: an empty pew with a hat on the seat.

**Per-card structure:** name (display type) · one-line description in Wendell's voice · ABV · key botanicals · label-art note · "where to buy" link to pews · price.

**Load-bearing nouns:** the six product names verbatim · ABVs as listed · botanical names from the canonical list.

**Voice sample (product description, PASS THE PLATE):**

> "Sunday morning. Golden in the glass. Bitter enough that you remember it was made for the offering bowl, not the tip jar. Drink it neat in a small glass before lunch."

**What NOT to include:** flowery tasting notes ("notes of vanilla orchid and crushed velvet") · star ratings · "award-winning" claims · stockphotographed bottle shots — every bottle photograph must look like a barn shelf or a hand-set still life.

## 5. hymns — recipes & cocktails

**Purpose:** show how to use the bottles. Bartender-friendly, but written in Wendell's voice, not the bartender's.

**Hero beat:** a hand-set page header *"HYMNS"* (display type) + one line: *"Five ways to drink it. Pick your pew."*

**Sections — five cocktails, each its own block:**

For each cocktail:

- **Name** — gospel / country-song coded.
- **Ingredients** — 4–5 lines, ingredients + measures.
- **Method** — 2–3 declarative sentences.
- **Note** — one short Wendell-voice line about when to drink it.

**Cocktail names (draft, workshop with the user):**

1. **The Wayfaring Stranger** — TENT REVIVAL + bourbon + bitter orange + bitters. Stirred. Neat. Sad.
2. **I Saw The Light** — PASS THE PLATE + Lillet Blanc + soda + lemon twist. Tall. Bright. Sunday-morning.
3. **Sunday Morning Coming Down** — THE BACKSLIDER + cold black coffee + cream. Built. Slow. (Kris Kristofferson reference.)
4. **The Folsom Old-Fashioned** — DEACON'S DRAUGHT + rye + a single drop of DRINK YE ALL OF IT (aromatic). Stirred. Single rock. (Johnny Cash reference.)
5. **The No-Show Spritz** — THE NO-SHOW + dry sparkling wine + soda + grapefruit peel. Spritz. Pink. For the pew you didn't sit in.

**Load-bearing nouns:** the six product names · cocktail names as listed (or workshopped equivalents).

**Voice sample (note line under "I Saw The Light"):**

> "Drink it on a porch before the heat of the day. Don't talk much."

**What NOT to include:** cocktail history lectures · "perfect for any occasion" copy · five-star ratings · branded glassware names. If a measurement needs explanation, the cocktail is wrong for this page.

**Hero beat:** one line: *"If you're in Asheville, try these pews."* (No image needed — type-only header is on-brand here.)

4. **Bars and shops elsewhere** — Brooklyn, Atlanta, Nashville, Charleston — short list, one or two each.
5. **For trade** — one line: "Pour Holler & Hymn at your bar? Text me." + Wendell's phone.

**Load-bearing nouns:** Asheville · placeholder bar names · Lexington Ave / River Arts District / South Slope as Asheville neighborhood markers · Marshall / Brevard / Black Mountain / Boone for WNC.

**Voice sample (per-bar one-liner):**

> "Sermon Bar — Lexington Ave. They pour the TENT REVIVAL neat in a Pyrex cup. Closest thing to a hymn."

**What NOT to include:** Yelp summaries · operating hours (link out) · maps (link out) · phone numbers for bars (the bars handle that).

## 7. visit — book a Saturday tasting

**Purpose:** invite people to the barn. The most "show up in person" page on the site. CTA-led.

**Hero beat:** a wide photograph of the barn at golden hour (Lane 1 imagery) + one line: *"Come to the barn. Saturdays only. Bring your own pew."*

**Sections:**

1. **What to expect** — Wendell pours four spirits. Tells the story of each. The tour is the still, the shed, the mason-jar shelf. ~90 minutes.
2. **When** — Saturdays, year-round, two seatings (2pm and 4pm). Closed for foraging weeks (he'll list them).
3. **What it costs** — $30 per person. Cash or text-to-pay. Children welcome but no pours.
4. **How to book** — text Wendell directly. No form, no platform. Just a phone number.
5. **How to get there** — Wolfpen Holler is on a dirt road. Specific driving notes (the last mile is unpaved; high-clearance helpful but not required).

**Load-bearing nouns:** Wolfpen Holler · Madison County · Saturdays · 2pm and 4pm · $30 · the barn · the still · the shed · the mason-jar shelf · 90 minutes.

**Voice sample (the "what to expect" close):**

> "I'll pour four spirits. I'll tell you about each one. If you want to ask questions, ask them. If you don't, that's fine too."

**What NOT to include:** booking forms with fifteen required fields · "luxurious tasting experience" copy · packages, tiers, or upsells · waivers (the disclaimers go in the footer).

## Boilerplate (legal / utility — not Wendell's voice)

- **Age-verification gate.** Standard 21+ block before the site loads. Branded — uses Holler & Hymn typography and palette. Two buttons: *"Yes, I am 21 or older"* and *"No, I am not."*
- **Privacy policy.** Short, plainspoken. One paragraph on cookies, one on what we collect (nothing if you don't sign up for anything), one on third parties (Shopify for the .shop subdomain only).
- **Shipping & legal.** Which US states we can ship to (the alcohol-shipping legal grid). Carrier handling. Adult-signature-required notice. Link to the .shop subdomain for actual transactions.

These can be in Wendell's voice if it doesn't break clarity — but accuracy and legal sufficiency override register. Keep boring where boring is required.
