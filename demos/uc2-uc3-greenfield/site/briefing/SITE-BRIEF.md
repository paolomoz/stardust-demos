# Holler & Hymn — Site brief

> One-page narrative brief. Frames the whole site in the language a designer or strategist would have written before the first page is drawn. Doubles as the draft input that UC3's `brief.pdf` will be derived from — the final demo prop reconciles this against the real `PRODUCT.md` Stardust produces.

---

## Premise

**Holler & Hymn** is a small-batch Appalachian botanical-liqueur house in Wolfpen Holler, Madison County, North Carolina — about forty minutes outside Asheville. Everything is made by one person — Wendell Greene — from herbs and fruit foraged or grown within forty miles of his barn. Amari, bitters, cordials, low-ABV aperitivi. Hand-distilled in a copper still Wendell built in 2019. Hand-labeled in a shed he bought off a dead preacher. No synthetic aromas, no industrial shortcuts, nothing bought in a jug.

The brand is the **ampersand**. The HOLLER — outlaw, place-rooted, foraged, hand-made — and the HYMN — heritage, ritual, revival-tent language — at full volume on top of each other. Saturday-night sin and Sunday-morning hymn in the same bottle. Every line of copy and every visual decision on the site should sit on both sides at once.

## Audience

Three concentric circles:

1. **Asheville-area drinkers, bartenders, and stockists** — the locals who can show up at the barn for a Saturday tasting and reorder by texting Wendell.
2. **Craft-spirits-curious visitors** — tourists in the WNC mountains looking for makers worth visiting, plus Hudson Valley / Brooklyn / Portland transplants who already buy Faccia Brutto / Forthave / St. Agrestis and want a Southern equivalent.
3. **Bars and restaurants** outside the region looking to carry a regional amaro with a real story.

The site is the *brand* destination, not the *shop*. Transaction is offloaded to a separate Shopify subdomain. The main site exists to make the audience repeat a line from it.

## What success looks like

The reader leaves with **one line they can repeat**. Candidates:

- "Forty miles. Twenty-seven herbs. One preacher's shed."
- "Saturday night sin and Sunday morning hymn. Both in one bottle."
- "Made by hand. From a to z."

Secondary: the reader can name the maker (Wendell), the place (Wolfpen Holler), and at least one product (PASS THE PLATE / TENT REVIVAL / THE BACKSLIDER).

## Voice and mode

- **Single-maker first-person.** Wendell is the narrator everywhere except utility pages. Never "we / our team."
- **Plainspoken declaratives.** Periods. Short sentences. Numbers and proper nouns over adjectives.
- **Revival vocabulary plays sparingly.** Sacred/profane wordplay is the brand's irreverence engine, but each instance must land — not every line, not every section.
- **Cheek lives in product names and label art, not Wendell's voice.** Wendell himself sounds tired and right. The labels are where the brand winks.

Full vocabulary and don'ts in `../brand/BRAND.md`.

## The five things that must be on the site

If we strip the site to its load-bearing minimum:

1. **The maker.** Wendell Greene, by name, with the origin paragraph.
2. **The place.** Wolfpen Holler, Madison County, forty miles around Asheville. With landmarks named.
3. **The process.** Forage → macerate → distill → label → bottle. Each step with one specific number or detail that proves the literalism.
4. **The line.** The six initial products, with names front-and-center and minimal description. Names do most of the work.
5. **One way to act.** Either "find a bar that pours it" (pews) or "book a Saturday tasting" (visit). Single CTA per page.

Everything else is optional.

## Tone reference

**Yes:** old WNC fruit-crate labels · 1940s revival-tent posters · vintage Appalachian state-fair signage · *The Foxfire Book* typography · country-music album sleeves from the early-70s outlaw era · hand-painted plywood roadside signs on US-25 north of Asheville.

**No:** Kinfolk / Cereal-Magazine luxe-minimal-craft · Brooklyn industrial-craft Edison-bulb subway-tile · Wes Anderson costume-symmetrical pastel · Etsy folk-craft hand-drawn-fern-in-dusty-rose · doormat country signage · modern craft-beer label illustration.

Full anti-patterns in `../brand/BRAND.md`.

## Format and constraints

- **Single-page scroll narrative as the home page.** Hero, manifesto, the holler, the still, the bottles, where to find, contact — one long scroll. Deep pages expand each section.
- **Nine pages total** (home + 8 deep). See `CONTENT.md` for per-page briefs.
- **Age-verification gate.** Legal requirement (alcohol). Branded, not generic — Wendell's voice on the gate too.
- **Catalog PDF as paper-artifact CTA.** Downloadable, free, no email gate. Mirrors the maker grammar.
- **Shop is offloaded.** External Shopify subdomain (e.g. `holler-hymn.shop`). The brand site links out; doesn't transact.
