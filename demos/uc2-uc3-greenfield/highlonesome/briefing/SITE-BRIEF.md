# High Lonesome — Site brief

> One-page narrative brief. Input to the would-be `/stardust:brief`. Frames the whole site.

---

## Premise

**High Lonesome** is a small-batch botanical-spirits house in Hot Springs, Madison County, North Carolina — a barn ten miles past anywhere. Made by Sadie Greene: a fiddle player who spent a summer in northern Italy in 1983 and came back with a permanent sense of color. Three bottles: a cobalt-tinged aperitif (*Blue Ridge Blue*), a sharp high-elevation amaro (*The Gap*), a dark wild-persimmon cordial (*Deep Cove*). The brand sound is mountain-music single-voice; the brand surface is Italian-summer saturated palette. The two halves are the brand.

## Audience

Three audiences:

1. **WNC drinkers and bartenders** — locals who can drive to Hot Springs for a Saturday tasting and reorder by text. Primary.
2. **Out-of-state mountain-music enthusiasts** — bluegrass festival regulars, Berea folkways audience, Smithsonian Folkways subscribers, the *Hangout Music Festival* / *Bristol Rhythm & Roots* crowd. Order online through the shop.
3. **Bartenders elsewhere in the Southeast** — Asheville, Greenville, Chattanooga, Knoxville, Charlottesville. Trade buyers who carry single-maker regional amaro.

Site is brand storytelling; transactional shop offloaded to `highlonesome.shop`.

## What success looks like

The reader leaves with **one line**. Candidates:

- *"One voice in a wide place."*
- *"Bottled bluegrass."*
- *"I sing while I work. I think it goes in."*

Secondary: the reader can name the maker (Sadie Greene), the place (Hot Springs, NC / a barn ten miles past anywhere), and the central duality (mountain music × Italian color).

## Voice and mode

- Single-maker first-person (Sadie). Never "we."
- Slow declaratives. Short sentences.
- Specifics over modifiers.
- Reverent, not irreverent. The brand is *joyful* not *playful*. Holler & Hymn winks; High Lonesome smiles.
- Italian-DNA stays implicit. No Italian language in copy.

## The five things that must be on the site

1. **The maker.** Sadie. Her origin and the 1983 Italian summer.
2. **The place.** Hot Springs, NC. The gaps and balds. The barn ten miles past anywhere.
3. **The sound.** The high-lonesome sound — Bill Monroe's coined phrase, what's in the bottle.
4. **The bottles.** Three: Blue Ridge Blue, The Gap, Deep Cove. Saturated labels.
5. **Where to find.** Bars in WNC + the .shop subdomain.

## Tone reference

**Yes:** Smithsonian Folkways album sleeves · *The Foxfire Book* typography (typographically) · saturated mid-century Italian travel posters · Bill Monroe's "Live at the Cellar Door" · Massimo Vignelli's grid · Olafur Eliasson's saturated atmospheric installations · Wes Anderson when he restrains himself.

**No:** Kinfolk luxe-minimal · Brooklyn industrial-craft · craft-beer-can illustration · doormat-country signage · trattoria checkered-tablecloth · SaaS template hero.

## Format and constraints

- **Cinematic landscape-led home page** as primary mode. Single long scroll with atmospheric image+text sections. Per-section ground colors vary across the saturated palette (per the user's documented saturation preference — diverse, broad-set, asymmetric).
- **Seven pages total** (home + 6 deep). Home / The Song (maker) / The Gaps (place + still merged) / The Bottle (three products with full pages) / Shop (link out) / Hymns (recipes & cocktails) / Contact.
- **Age-verification gate.** Required (21+).
- **Catalog as a downloaded PDF** is OPTIONAL for High Lonesome (unlike Holler & Hymn, where it was load-bearing). Sadie's brand doesn't depend on a paper artifact.
- **Shop offloaded** to `highlonesome.shop`.
