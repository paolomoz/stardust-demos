# Chapitô — Brand definition

> Source document for `/stardust:seed`. Drives the brand half of `stardust/current/` (palette, typography, voice, motif catalog, **compositional motif catalog**).
> Companion to `briefing/SITE-BRIEF.md` (`/stardust:brief` input).
>
> **Reference visual register:** the brand is consciously modelled on the maximalist Italian-pop collage register exemplified by [opificiocattaneo.com](https://opificiocattaneo.com/) — sunburst medallion hero, X-crossed marquee ribbons, highlighter-tape phrase pulls inside prose, vivid full-bleed color-block product sections with cutout-photo collage, ticket-stub corner tags, hand-painted display type. **The visual moves are the brand's load-bearing surface;** see § Compositional motif catalog. The cultural register is rotated to Lisbon (circus + Tagus + Alfama + Alentejo) so no Italian-language or Italian-place reference leaks. Anti-references include "Italian liquor-maker pastiche" explicitly.

---

## Identity at a glance

**Name:** **Chapitô** (with the diacritic; the circumflex is load-bearing — it is the tent peak).
**Tagline:** *Molhos com piruetas. Casa pequena, picante grande.*
**Category:** Small-batch Portuguese hot sauces — piri-piri, fermentations, smoked sweet-fires.
**Place:** A storefront-workshop on Costa do Castelo, Alfama, Lisbon. The peppers come from a family patch in Alentejo, ten minutes outside Estremoz.
**Maker:** **Inês Bento**. Sole proprietor. Former trapeze understudy at the Chapitô collective up the hill; bottles sauces in the same building she used to fall onto crash mats.

## Central tension (the brand's load-bearing move)

**Big-top spectacle × Alentejo slow-cook.**

The brand's *flavor* is the slow patience of the Alentejo — peppers grown a year, dried two months, fermented six weeks, cooked in copper. Sun, salt, time. That's what's *in* the bottle.

The brand's *surface* is fairground spectacle — circus barker maximalism, hand-painted signage, ticket-stub graphics, marquee ribbons, sunburst medallions, color-blocked product collages. *Loud, vivid, paper-poster, hand-drawn.* That's what's *on* the bottle.

Inês trained as a trapeze understudy for three years at the Chapitô circus school up the hill in Alfama (Costa do Castelo, 1996–1999). She came back to her family's pepper patch near Estremoz, started bottling the family piri-piri at twenty-six, and dressed the entire brand in fairground clothes the day she opened the storefront in 2018. The brand is the food her grandmother made (slow, patient, Alentejo-rural, single-recipe) wearing the clothes she wore on the trapeze (loud, painted, big-top, all-spectacle).

**The tension reads as:** A 1962 Portuguese travelling-circus poster wheat-pasted over the door of a family pepper-curing shed in the Alentejo countryside. Fairground over farm. Both halves at full volume.

Strip either half and the brand collapses:

- Pure Alentejo-farmhouse aesthetic (terracotta, hand-thrown ceramics, beige linen, *Cereal Magazine* country-luxe): becomes a Slow Food retreat brochure. Tasteful, predictable, indistinguishable from every other "honest small-batch Portuguese" project.
- Pure fairground-spectacle aesthetic without the farm content: becomes a costume-shop brand. Detached from the food that earns the spectacle.

Both halves at full volume = the brand.

## The maker — Inês Bento

**Origin paragraph** (verbatim or near-verbatim on the about page):

> "I learned to fall in Alfama and to grow peppers in Estremoz. The Chapitô was a big top on Costa do Castelo where I trained from sixteen to nineteen — three years on the trapeze, none of them good enough. So I went home. My avó had a piri-piri patch behind the curing shed; I started bottling. Twenty-six when I opened the storefront in 2018, same hill where I used to fall. Casa pequena, picante grande. The labels are loud because the kitchen is quiet."
> — Inês

**Tone calibration:**

- Mostly first-person singular, present tense. Short declaratives in Portuguese rhythm — the English copy follows the same cadence.
- Specifics ("Costa do Castelo", "Estremoz", "six weeks", "twenty-six bottles in the first batch") over modifiers.
- Cheek is allowed, never costume. The brand is *playful* (sauces named after circus figures) AND *reverent* (the food is from her grandmother's recipe and that part is sacred). Both at once.
- Portuguese language present but never costume — anchor phrases, product names, address blocks. NEVER tourist-Portuguese phrases (`obrigada`, `fado`, `saudade` are forbidden as decoration). Portuguese is the brand's native language, used as native speakers use it, not sprinkled.

## Voice & copy rules

### Do

- First-person singular. *I, my, me — Eu, o meu, a minha.*
- Plainspoken short declaratives. Periods over commas.
- Specifics ("Costa do Castelo, número 7", "ano após ano", "seis semanas de fermentação", "frasco de 100 ml") over modifiers.
- One-line aphorisms when needed — *"Molhos com piruetas. Casa pequena, picante grande."*
- Portuguese product names always in Portuguese, never translated in the copy itself — translation lives in a small mono italic gloss line below.

### Don't

- "We" / "our team" / "passionate" / "crafted" / "curated" / "artisanal" / "handcrafted" / "small-batch with care." Forbidden.
- *Saudade* / *fado* / *azulejo* / *bacalhau* / *pastel de nata* used as decoration or atmosphere. Forbidden. (They can appear when literally relevant — a sauce that pairs with bacalhau may say so — but never as mood-setting nouns.)
- Italian-language phrases or Italian-place references of any kind. The reference's cultural register is the surface inversion this brand inverts AWAY from; nothing Italian should appear.
- Adjective stacks of three or more.
- Irreverence-as-default. The brand winks at the spectacle and is reverent about the food. Don't wink at the food.
- Translation-as-condescension. The copy is bilingual when it's bilingual; do not parenthesise translations as if the reader were a tourist.

### Voice anchors (lift verbatim where useful)

- *"Molhos com piruetas."* (Sauces with pirouettes.)
- *"Casa pequena, picante grande."* (Small house, big spice.)
- *"Cada frasco, uma figura."* (Each jar, a figure.)
- *"As etiquetas são barulhentas porque a cozinha é silenciosa."* (The labels are loud because the kitchen is quiet.)
- *"Vem ao Chapitô."* (Come to the Chapitô.)
- *"O sol num frasco."* (The sun in a jar.)
- *"Trapézio sobre malagueta."* (Trapeze over chili.)
- *"Comprado na loja, feito na casa."* (Sold in the store, made in the house.)

## Visual identity

### Palette (the 10 tokens — brand-native naming)

```css
/* Brand-native names connect the fairground-pop palette to Lisbon + Alentejo */
--chapito-cravo:         #f4b09d;  /* dominant peach-pink ground; cravo = carnation, also the April Revolution flower */
--chapito-areia:         #fbe9d0;  /* warm cream; sand at Caparica beach */
--chapito-piri:          #ed7f3f;  /* primary orange; piri-piri ripe-red-orange */
--chapito-laranja-doce:  #e95b27;  /* hotter orange; sweet-orange of a circus poster */
--chapito-azulejo:       #3fbcaf;  /* turquoise teal; Lisbon tile, also Tejo morning light */
--chapito-galinha:       #f3c84e;  /* yolk mustard; the colour of farmhouse chicken-skin */
--chapito-malagueta:     #9b3536;  /* brick / burgundy; ripe-malagueta-chili colour */
--chapito-tejo:          #1f4548;  /* deep teal-navy; Tagus at dusk; the brand's dark surface */
--chapito-noite:         #1a1410;  /* near-black ink; barn-floor ink, never pure #000 */
--chapito-cal:           #fffaef;  /* limewash white; Alfama wall whitewash, never pure #fff */
```

**Token rules:**

1. **`chapito-cravo` is the dominant surface.** Most of the brand's chrome lives here — the hero ground, the marquee band, the footer band, the manifesto band. The peach is the brand's *page* in the same way `chapito-tejo` is the brand's *cover*.
2. **Four product-section grounds, one per family — never repeat.** Each product family gets its own vivid ground, used ONCE in the long scroll: `chapito-piri` (orange) for Piri-Piri family · `chapito-galinha` (mustard) for Doce Fogo family · `chapito-cravo` reused with a high-saturation overlay for Equilibrista family · `chapito-tejo` (deep teal) for Cabeçudo family. No product section may use the same ground as another product section.
3. **`chapito-tejo` is the cover-of-night.** Used once as a full-bleed bottle-hero section (the flagship Trapézio block) and as the very-bottom legal-footer band. Never as body-paragraph ground.
4. **`chapito-malagueta` is the burgundy accent only.** Used for ticket-stub corner tags, highlighter-rectangle pulls in body prose, occasional outlined display. Never as a section ground.
5. **`chapito-noite` is the deepest text colour.** Body, mono micro-labels on light grounds, marquee text. Never pure `#000000`.
6. **`chapito-cal` is the limewash white.** Marquee ribbons, white-tape bands, light text on dark grounds. Never pure `#ffffff`.

**Anti-tokens (forbidden in this brand):**

- Pure `#000000` and pure `#ffffff` — too digital for a hand-painted brand.
- Sage / charcoal / oatmeal / beige neutrals — collapses the brand into Slow-Food / *Cereal Magazine* terracotta-luxe.
- Pastel-pink ≠ `chapito-cravo`. The brand's pink is a saturated peach-coral, not a Wes-Anderson candy pastel.
- Synthetic purples, magentas, hot-pinks — outside the palette.
- Tile-blue cobalt — would read as `azulejo` decoration; the brand's blue is teal-deep `chapito-tejo`, not Royal-Lisbon-tile cobalt.

### Typography

Three tiers + one logotype:

1. **Display — bold condensed all-caps sans.** Anton (free), Druk Condensed, or Acumin Bold Condensed at huge scale. Used for **section titles** ("PIRI-PIRI", "DOCE FOGO", "EQUILIBRISTA"), **product family banners**, **CTA buttons** at primary scale. Tracking tight (-0.01em). Mixed-case display is forbidden — the brand's display tier is unapologetically all-caps fairground-poster.
2. **Body — humanist sans.** Inter, Outfit, or DM Sans at 17–19px, line-height 1.55. For prose, descriptions, hover copy. Roman default; italic for emphasis (e.g. the highlighter-tape spans).
3. **Mono — IBM Plex Mono.** Micro-labels, dates, ABV, ml, badges, ticket-stub corner tags ("Nº 01", "VOL. I", "EST. 2018"), numerals throughout body when measurables appear.
4. **Logotype (one usage, NOT a freely deployable face) — classical serif spaced wide in a circle around a central monogrammatic C.** Bodoni Display or Playfair Display, weight 400, tracking +0.18em, set as circular type. Used ONLY in the medallion wordmark (hero + footer + favicon). Never used outside the medallion.

**Type rules:**

- Display is **all-caps by default**. Mixed-case display is forbidden.
- Body is roman by default; italic is reserved for highlighter-tape pulls and verbatim Inês-voice quotes.
- Numerals always render in mono ("100 ml", "8% ABV doesn't apply — these are sauces, ABV ≠ relevant", "EST. 2018", "Nº 01", "+351 ...").
- Script faces, brush faces, hand-lettered display faces are FORBIDDEN as standing text. They appear only as raster artwork on bottle labels (Lane 3 imagery), never as web type. The "hand-lettered" feel of the brand comes from the *all-caps condensed display* + the *medallion circular serif*, never from a script web-font.

### Imagery direction (three lanes)

**Lane 1 — Cutout B&W vintage photography (LOAD-BEARING).** Black-and-white photographs of Portuguese street life, mid-century — sardine fishermen, Alfama-balcony grandmothers, Lisbon tram drivers, beach photographers at Caparica, fairground performers from old travelling-circus archives. Each image is **cut out from its background** (transparent surround) and floats over a solid-color section ground. The cutout-collage register is the brand's signature treatment; replaces the "cinematic landscape" lane that most artisan brands default to. Each color-block product section carries 3–4 of these cutouts arranged at off-axis angles, NOT laid out in a grid.

**Lane 2 — Hand-painted bottle labels.** Each product carries a hand-painted, screen-printed-look label in palette tones. Bold flat areas, hand-cut typography (raster, not web), single signature illustration per product (a trapeze artist for Trapézio, a fakir on a bed of chili for Faquir, a fire-eater for Cuspidor de Fogo). Cardinal motifs: the circus figure + a chili silhouette + a hand-drawn medallion.

**Lane 3 — Workshop process + Alentejo patch photography (used sparingly).** Color photos — the copper pot, the curing shed, the pepper patch in late summer, hands at work, jars in the cooling rack. Used as secondary atmospheric inserts ONLY in the about / process pages. NEVER in the home-page color-block product sections, which are all Lane 1 cutout-collage.

## Compositional motif catalog — the brand's load-bearing surface

This section is the brand's design-systems-level differentiator. **Every motif here MUST be reimplemented in any prototype** — they are the brand's surface, not stylistic options.

### Motif 1 — Sunburst medallion hero

A full-viewport hero on `chapito-cravo` ground with **radial alternating-ray sunburst** in `chapito-areia` / `chapito-cravo-tint` covering the upper viewport (16 rays, alternating). At the center: a **circular wordmark medallion** in `chapito-cal` containing a large monogrammatic `C` in classical serif, with `CHAPITÔ` arcing top, `MOLHOS FEITOS À MÃO` arcing bottom (both in spaced-wide classical serif). Around the medallion, **6 product bottles arranged in a circle** at clock positions 12 / 2 / 4 / 6 / 8 / 10. Each bottle is a Lane 2 hand-painted-label rendering. The whole sunburst feels like a fairground spinning-wheel frozen at high noon.

Top-left and top-right corners carry **ticket-stub tags** — small rectangular plates rotated −6° / +6°, each with a B&W cutout photo (Lane 1) and a product-family label in mono all-caps (`PIRI-PIRI`, `EQUILIBRISTA`). Reads like two carnival tickets pinned to the medallion poster.

### Motif 2 — X-crossed marquee ribbon band

A horizontal band of `chapito-cravo` carrying **two diagonally-crossed white tape ribbons** that span edge-to-edge, intersecting in the center. Each ribbon repeats a phrase in `chapito-noite` Anton-style condensed all-caps, with hand-drawn `·` dot separators: `VEM AO CHAPITÔ · VEM AO CHAPITÔ · VEM AO CHAPITÔ` and `PROVA A CASA · PROVA A CASA · PROVA A CASA`. At the X intersection, a **circular button** in `chapito-tejo` ground / `chapito-cal` text reads `VAI À LOJA` — sits centred on the intersection like a button pinned through both tape ribbons. The ribbons rotate ~±12° from horizontal.

### Motif 3 — Highlighter-tape manifesto

A centered free-running paragraph in `chapito-noite` on `chapito-cravo` ground, ~7 lines, the brand's manifesto in Inês's voice. **5–7 random phrase-spans throughout the paragraph are each wrapped in a hard-edged, solid-color highlighter rectangle** — flat fill, NO gradient, NO drop shadow, NO rounded corners (≤ 2px). The highlight colours rotate through `chapito-azulejo`, `chapito-piri`, `chapito-galinha`, `chapito-malagueta`, `chapito-tejo` (white text on the tejo span). The highlighter rectangles are the brand's signature ornament inside prose — they are NOT optional, NOT decorative; they are the brand reading itself out loud.

Below the paragraph, a single mono-label CTA button: `▸ DESCARREGA O CATÁLOGO COMPLETO`.

### Motif 4 — Color-block product family section (×4)

Stacked vertically below the manifesto. Each is a full-bleed band on its own vivid ground (orange · mustard · pink · deep-teal — one per family, never repeat). Each section is composed of:

- **6 bottles in a single horizontal row** across the visual middle of the section, evenly spaced, each at ~140px height — Lane 2 hand-painted labels.
- **3–4 B&W cutout vintage photos** (Lane 1) floating around the bottle row at off-axis angles (e.g. a grandmother's portrait rotated -8° upper-left, a vintage car rotated +5° upper-right, a fairground-performer profile rotated -3° lower-left, a balcony-laundry-line cutout rotated +9° lower-right). The cutouts overlap the bottle row at the edges, layering depth without using drop shadows.
- A **diagonal stripe accent block** bottom-right corner: a rectangle filled with alternating diagonal stripes in `chapito-cal` + a contrasting accent (`chapito-piri` for the orange section, `chapito-cravo` for the mustard section, etc.). ~120×80px, rotated ~15°.
- A **polka-dot accent rectangle** bottom-left: ~120×80px, light dots on a lighter ground in cool-accent tone.
- Top-center: bold condensed all-caps display family name in `chapito-noite` (or `chapito-cal` for the deep-teal section) at 64–80px. Below it, a 3-term mono micro-label row separated by `|`: `PIRI-PIRI | 100% NATURAL | DOIS ANOS DE FOGO`.

The four sections, in order:
1. **PIRI-PIRI** — `chapito-piri` ground (vivid orange). Sauces: Trapézio · Faquir · Equilibrista · Domador · Saltimbanco · Cuspidor de Fogo.
2. **DOCE FOGO** — `chapito-galinha` ground (mustard). Sweeter, fruit-forward: Palhaço · Contorcionista · Acrobata · Funâmbulo · Mestre-de-Cerimónias · Malabarista.
3. **EQUILIBRISTA / FERMENTADOS** — `chapito-cravo` ground with cyan overlay (saturated pink-cyan). Balanced, fermented, umami: Trapézio Negro · Funâmbulo Verde · Acrobata Cinco-Especiarias · Saltimbanco Cítrico · Equilibrista (the eponym) · Contorcionista Misô.
4. **CABEÇUDO / NOVIDADES** — `chapito-tejo` ground (deep teal — section displays light type on dark). Experimental, novelty: Cabeçudo · Anão Sábio · Mulher-Barbuda · Homem-Forte · Diretor do Circo · Bilheteira.

### Motif 5 — Chip-tag cluster (single product family)

The **EQUILIBRISTA / FERMENTADOS** section also carries a **chip-tag cluster** between the bottle row and the bottom accents: 6 small **circular cutout-photo chips** (each ~60×60px circular crop of a B&W Lane 1 portrait or face) with a colored half-band across the bottom 40% of the chip carrying the product name in tiny mono all-caps (`EQUILIBRISTA`, `FAQUIR`, `PALHAÇO`). The chips sit in a loose horizontal cluster, slightly overlapping, rotated at random angles (-4° to +6°). This motif is what the reference uses for its third-section family — it's about making the sauces feel like a *cast of figures*, not a *catalog of products*.

### Motif 6 — Big-dark bottle hero (the cover-of-night)

Full-bleed `chapito-tejo` section, ~80vh tall. Single flagship bottle (Trapézio) centered, ~480px tall. Behind the bottle, a **hand-drawn wave-line motif** in `chapito-cal` outline — three parallel sine-wave curves rendered as 2px strokes, evoking the Tagus at dusk (the brand's name is *Chapitô*, the place is the Tagus; the wave is the brand's water motif).

To the left of the bottle: large bold condensed all-caps in `chapito-cal` reading `TRAPÉZIO`, with a script-form raster lockup of the same word as a label-style stamp below it (rendered as SVG or PNG to avoid using a script web font). Right of the bottle: a 3-line mono micro-block — `LARGO DO CHAFARIZ · COSTA DO CASTELO · ALFAMA, LISBOA`. Below the bottle: a single italic-Spectral pull-anchor `O molho de assinatura. Trapézio sobre malagueta.`

### Motif 7 — Circular wordmark footer

Mirrors the hero medallion. Centered alone on `chapito-cravo` ground, ~200×200px circular wordmark medallion. Below it, a single row of 4 icon-links (email · Instagram · WhatsApp · phone), each rendered as a small mono icon + mono uppercase label.

### Motif 8 — Persistent thin bottom bar

Anchored to the viewport bottom (fixed). `chapito-cal` ground, ~36px tall. Left: mono all-caps `VISITA A LOJA` with a small arrow. Right: mono all-caps `VAI ATÉ LÁ →` linking to the shop subdomain. The bar is `position: fixed; bottom: 0` and sits on every page.

### Composition rules (apply across all sections)

- **No drop shadows. No gradients-as-elevation. No glassmorphism.** Depth comes from layering flat shapes — cutout collage, diagonal stripes, polka-dots, off-axis rotation.
- **Off-axis rotation everywhere.** Cutout photos, ticket-stub tags, marquee ribbons, accent rectangles, chip-tag cluster — they all sit at angles between -12° and +12°. Pure orthogonal layout is forbidden except where rhythm demands it (centered display, centered manifesto paragraph).
- **No SaaS silhouette.** No centered massive type + dual-CTA pair. No 3-col card grid for products. No 5/7 hero image-text split. The brand's silhouette is fairground-poster, not product-marketing.
- **Cutout-collage over photographic backgrounds.** Section grounds are SOLID color. Photography lives ON TOP of the ground as cutout layers, never as full-bleed photographic backdrop with overlaid type.
- **Padding alternates 96px / 64px / 96px to break rhythm.** Same rule as High Lonesome — the saturated palette reads more saturated when the rhythm is asymmetric.
- **Mode A pinned.** Palette and type tier locked. No new font outside the 4-family deck (Bodoni for medallion · Anton for display · Inter for body · IBM Plex Mono for mono).

- **Piri-Piri** — classic sharp red hot sauces. Six bottles: Trapézio (flagship) · Faquir · Equilibrista · Domador · Saltimbanco · Cuspidor de Fogo. Section ground: `chapito-piri`.
- **Doce Fogo** — sweeter, fruit-forward hot sauces. Six bottles: Palhaço · Contorcionista · Acrobata · Funâmbulo · Mestre-de-Cerimónias · Malabarista. Section ground: `chapito-galinha`.
- **Equilibrista / Fermentados** — balanced, fermented, umami. Six bottles: Trapézio Negro · Funâmbulo Verde · Acrobata Cinco-Especiarias · Saltimbanco Cítrico · Equilibrista · Contorcionista Misô. Section ground: `chapito-cravo` with cyan overlay.

## Anti-references

- **Italian liquor-maker pastiche of any kind.** No `Opificio` / `Liquoreria` / `Amaro` / `Aperitivo` / `Cattaneo`. The reference brand is the visual register inversion this brand inverts AWAY from. The cultural register is Lisbon + Alentejo, never Italian.
- **Slow-Food / Cereal-Magazine / *Kinfolk* country-luxe.** Terracotta + linen + hand-thrown ceramic + sage neutrals = the brand's loudest no.
- **Tourist-Lisbon clichés.** No *azulejo* tile imagery, no *fado* references in copy, no *pastel de nata* shorthand, no *bacalhau* as set-dressing. The brand lives in Lisbon as a native, not as a postcard.
- **Costume-Portuguese.** No `obrigado` plate, no anchor-and-rope nautical pastiche, no `Lisbon` graffiti type.
- **Wes Anderson costume-symmetric pastel.** The brand is asymmetric, off-axis, hand-drawn. NOT Anderson-symmetric.
- **Etsy folk-craft / "homestead-spicy" Americana.** No mason-jar tags, no kraft-paper labels, no chalkboard signage.
- **Brooklyn industrial-craft.** No Edison bulb, no subway tile, no Helvetica-on-blackboard.
- **Modern craft-beer label vernacular.** The brand is Portuguese fairground-poster, not Williamsburg can-art.
- **Generic-2026-SaaS silhouette.** No centered hero + dual-CTA pair + balanced padding + serial footer.
- **Editorial-register vocabulary in chrome.** Never *atelier*, *the studio*, *mise-en-place*, *the journal*, *dispatches*.
- **Hand-lettered web fonts.** No script faces (Pacifico, Permanent Marker, Reenie Beanie) as standing text. Hand-lettering appears only as raster on bottle labels.
- **Drop shadows, gradients-as-elevation, glassmorphism, neumorphism.** Forbidden universally.
- **Italian-language words anywhere in copy.** Forbidden. Portuguese only (and English where bilingual makes sense).

## Logo direction

**Principle:** when the brand is seeded against a reference, the logo must differ from the reference's logo *more* than other compositional moves. Other surface vocabulary can be lifted; the logo is identity-specific and must read as Chapitô's own mark, not a re-skin.

**Form:** big-top tent silhouette as the logo frame — a frontal triangular tent shape with vertical stripes (cravo + cal alternating) inside, a scalloped malagueta base flap, a small pennant flag rising from the apex, and the wordmark CHAPITÔ stamped inside the tent in bold condensed all-caps (Anton). A short mono sub-line sits below the tent: `MOLHOS · À MÃO · LISBOA · 2018`.

**The circumflex pun is preserved but relocated.** Instead of a `^` floating above a monogrammatic C, the tent itself IS the ô-shape (rounded crown over a base), and the small pennant rising from the apex visually echoes the `^` diacritic as a flag silhouette. Triple visual rhyme: the pennant, the tent peak, and the ô-in-CHAPITÔ.

**Variants:**
- Primary: `chapito-noite` strokes and wordmark on `chapito-cravo` tent fill, with `chapito-cal` stripes and `chapito-malagueta` pennant + flap.
- Reversed: same construction with `chapito-cal` strokes/wordmark on `chapito-tejo` ground.
- Single-color: line-only outline for embossed / single-tone applications.

**Prompt seed for external generation** (Gemini Lane 2 / illustrator handoff):

> "A flat, hand-printed-feel logo of a circus big-top tent silhouette, viewed straight-on. Triangular tent with three vertical stripes inside (alternating warm peach and cream), thin dark ink outline, and a scalloped burgundy base flap. A small pennant flag with a triangular cutout (like a circumflex accent) rises from the apex of the tent on a thin pole. The word 'CHAPITÔ' is set inside the tent in bold condensed all-caps sans-serif (Anton-style), dark ink. Below the tent in a small mono uppercase sub-line: 'MOLHOS · À MÃO · LISBOA · 2018'. Two color variants: dark ink on warm peach ground; warm cream on deep teal ground. Hand-printed letterpress / silkscreen feel, not slick vector. No additional ornament beyond two small star marks framing the wordmark."

## Closing reminders for the prototype stage

The motifs in § Compositional motif catalog are non-negotiable. A render of Chapitô that lacks the sunburst medallion hero, the X-crossed marquee, the highlighter-tape manifesto, the four color-block sections with cutout-collage, the chip-tag cluster, the big-dark bottle hero, OR the medallion footer is not Chapitô — it's another small-batch sauces brand site with the right palette. Token substitution alone produces slop. The motifs are the brand.

When `/stardust:prototype` renders the home page, the brief must enumerate each motif by name and the render must reimplement it (in real CSS / real layout, NOT copy-pasted from the reference). Treat the reference as a vocabulary of moves, not a template.
