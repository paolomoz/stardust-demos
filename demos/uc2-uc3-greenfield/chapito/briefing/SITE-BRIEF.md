# Chapitô — Site brief

> One-page narrative brief. Input to the would-be `/stardust:brief`. Frames the whole site.

---

## Premise

**Chapitô** is a small-batch Portuguese hot-sauce house in Alfama, Lisbon — a storefront-workshop on Costa do Castelo, Nº 7. Made by Inês Bento: a former trapeze understudy at the Chapitô circus collective up the hill, who came home to her family's pepper patch outside Estremoz and started bottling sauces at twenty-six. Four product families: classic *Piri-Piri*, sweet-fire *Doce Fogo*, fermented *Equilibrista*, novelty *Cabeçudo*. The brand's flavor is Alentejo slow-cook; the brand's surface is Lisbon big-top spectacle. The two halves are the brand.

## Audience

Three audiences:

1. **Lisbon home cooks and restaurant chefs** — locals who can walk to Costa do Castelo for a Saturday tasting and reorder by WhatsApp. Primary.
2. **Out-of-town Portuguese food enthusiasts** — Porto + Algarve + Madeira + Açores customers, plus the Lisbon diaspora abroad. Order online through the shop subdomain.
3. **Restaurant trade buyers across Portugal and Spain** — Madrid, Barcelona, Sevilla, Faro, Porto. Restaurants that put a small-batch artisanal hot sauce on the table.

Site is brand storytelling; transactional shop offloaded to `chapito.shop`.

## What success looks like

The reader leaves with **one line**. Candidates:

- *"Molhos com piruetas."*
- *"Casa pequena, picante grande."*
- *"Cada frasco, uma figura."*
- *"As etiquetas são barulhentas porque a cozinha é silenciosa."*

Secondary: the reader can name the maker (Inês Bento), the place (Costa do Castelo, Alfama / pepper patch in Estremoz), and the central duality (Alentejo slow-cook × Lisbon big-top spectacle).

## Voice and mode

- Single-maker first-person (Inês). Never "we."
- Slow declaratives. Short sentences. Portuguese cadence (the English copy follows the same).
- Specifics over modifiers — "Costa do Castelo, número 7", "seis semanas", "ano após ano", "frasco de 100 ml".
- Reverent about the food, playful about the spectacle. Both at once.
- Bilingual where bilingual makes sense — product names in Portuguese, addresses in Portuguese, voice anchors in Portuguese; longer prose may be primarily Portuguese with English gloss in a smaller mono italic line below, or alternated section-by-section.

## The five things that must be on the site

1. **The maker.** Inês. The Chapitô trapeze years (1996–1999), the homecoming to Estremoz, the storefront opening in 2018.
2. **The place.** Costa do Castelo, Nº 7, Alfama, Lisbon. The pepper patch outside Estremoz. Both addresses on the page.
3. **The figures.** The brand's product line is a *cast* — each sauce is a circus figure with a name. The cast is the catalog.
4. **The four families.** Piri-Piri · Doce Fogo · Equilibrista · Cabeçudo. Each in its own ground color, each with a cast of six.
5. **Where to buy.** Restaurants that carry Chapitô in Lisbon and Porto + the `.shop` subdomain.

## Tone reference

**Yes:** Travelling-circus posters from 1950s–60s Portugal · hand-painted Lisbon shopfront signage (carved + painted, not vinyl) · Almodóvar saturated mid-century Iberian-pop · Stefan Sagmeister cut-paper collage · Lina Bo Bardi exhibition graphics · *Maria Bonita* and *Chita* fabric prints · Andre Carrilho cutout-collage illustration.

**No:** Slow Food / Cereal-Magazine country-luxe · Brooklyn industrial-craft · craft-beer-can illustration · Etsy folk-craft · tile-tourist Lisbon shorthand · Wes Anderson symmetric pastel · SaaS template hero · Italian liquor-maker pastiche.

## Format and constraints

- **Long-scroll cinematic home page** as primary mode. The home is the brand's manifesto + the four product families + the flagship-bottle hero — all in one scroll. Per-section ground colors deploy the saturated palette across distinct grounds (one per product family + the dominant peach + the deep teal once).
- **Seven pages total** (home + 6 deep). Home / A Casa (maker) / A Horta (place + process) / Os Molhos (full catalog) / Onde Provar (where to find) / Loja (link out) / Contacto.
- **Bilingual at the chrome.** Nav, footer, and CTA labels appear in Portuguese with English in smaller mono italic. Body text is primarily Portuguese on the about/process pages, English-led on the product catalog page for trade buyers. The home page is Portuguese-led with English glosses inline.
- **Persistent thin bottom bar.** `VISITA A LOJA → VAI ATÉ LÁ` band fixed at viewport bottom on every page.
- **Catalog as downloadable PDF — OPTIONAL.** A `▸ DESCARREGA O CATÁLOGO COMPLETO` link below the manifesto. Initial render may stub this.
- **No age-verification gate.** Hot sauces are not regulated alcohol; no gate needed (unlike High Lonesome).
- **Shop offloaded** to `chapito.shop`.

## Reference register

The visual register is modelled on a single concrete reference — **maximalist Italian-pop artisan-craft sites** in the lineage of [opificiocattaneo.com](https://opificiocattaneo.com/). The brand explicitly lifts the *vocabulary of compositional moves* (sunburst medallion, X-crossed marquee, highlighter-tape paragraph, color-block product collage with cutout B&W photography, chip-tag cluster, big-dark CTA bottle hero) and reimplements them in a Lisbon + circus cultural register. **No Italian language, no Italian place reference, no Italian liquor category, no specific reference-brand naming may appear in the implementation.** The reference is a vocabulary, not a template.

See `brand/BRAND.md § Compositional motif catalog` for the named-and-numbered list of moves the prototype is expected to reimplement.
