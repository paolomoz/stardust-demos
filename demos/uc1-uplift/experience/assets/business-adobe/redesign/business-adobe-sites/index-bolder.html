Adobe Experience Manager Sites.

Digital experience has evolved — your CMS should too. Experience Manager Sites addresses the core challenges of managing your digital properties, leveraging powerful agentic execution to help teams create, update, and activate experiences more efficiently. The result: structured, high-performing pages designed for both human engagement and AI visibility.

Content creation

Accelerate content creation with agentic capabilities.

Give marketers the ability to quickly create, update, and scale digital experiences faster with flexible authoring tools and built-in agentic support.

  • Modernize legacy content and use agentic workflows to automate updates, enforce governance and streamline optimization across sites.
  • Use flexible authoring to create and publish content directly from Microsoft Word and Google Docs, or use a visual editor for real time, in-context page editing.
  • Instantly create on-brand copy and image variations, test performance, and personalize content using integrated generative AI capabilities.
  • Reuse content across properties, manage metadata, and streamline translation to deliver consistent global experiences.

FAQs

  • Content personalization adapts the experience each visitor sees — copy, imagery, recommendations — based on profile, behavior, and intent. Experience Manager Sites makes this possible at scale by combining structured content, audience segmentation from Adobe Experience Platform, and agentic decisioning.

  • Content Services exposes managed content via APIs so that any front end — websites, apps, kiosks, AI agents — can render the same authoritative copy and assets without duplicating editorial work.

  • A headless CMS separates content management from presentation. Editors work in the CMS; developers consume the content through APIs and render it however they want — modern web frameworks, native apps, AI surfaces.

  • Content as a Service (CaaS) packages the structured authoring, governance, and delivery layer of Experience Manager Sites as a managed service — without the need to operate the underlying infrastructure.

  • Customer Experience is the sum of every interaction a customer has with a brand — across web, mobile, in-store, AI agents, and beyond. Experience Manager Sites is the structured content layer that makes those interactions consistent.

  • AEM Sites scales because it separates concerns: structured content, governance, performance, and delivery each run on dedicated, agentic services. Enterprises can add brands, regions, languages, and channels without rebuilding from scratch.
