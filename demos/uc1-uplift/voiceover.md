## Delivery notes

- **Neutral product voice.** No "I" or "we." Don't add asides or hedges in delivery — the writing is the asides.
- **"You must pick one."** at 0:23 — flat, dry. The joke is in the delivery, not the line.
- **"Unless you don't have to."** at 0:24 — pause before delivering. It's a pivot, not a punchline. Quiet, almost throwaway. The red-slab wipe does the work.
- **SLICC sequence (0:30–0:58).** Soft sequence markers — "Then…" "From there…" "And lands them…" — no numbered "step one / step two." Pace each with a short beat at the start so it tracks with the artifact swap.
- **"Same logo, same red, same brand."** at 1:00 — three-beat, no rush. Land each clause.
- **Beat 5 closer.** "*Same site, same brand, unique pages.*" — small pause before the final clause; it resolves the Beat 1 trap ("you must pick one"). That's the load-bearing thesis line for VP+ audience.
- **Beat 6.** "This is Stardust." — confident, low. "And BTW — it also crafted this demo." — small conversational shrug, drop the volume slightly, like an afterthought. The on-screen splash carries *"Math, not mysticism."* as the visual payoff; the VO doesn't echo it.
- **Don't echo on-screen text.** Lines like "Same brand. New surface." and "Not a project. A capability." live on screen — the VO routes around them so the audience processes both as one thought rather than reading-while-listening fatigue.
